# Chapter 1 - Whisperwoods

## Whispers

From above, the rustling of leaves as they shield the understory from the cold breeze that blows over the forest roof atop him. From below, the soft crunching of shed leaves beneath his horse's hooves. His eyes felt puffy from lack of sleep; he'd been riding for hours. For better or for worse, what keeps him alert are those sounds near-constantly ringing throughout the forest, like whispers and whistles - the region was certainly aptly named. He'd heard they come from these odd-looking oak trees, with branches that grow like copies of one another, and stalks along their twisting trunks and branches that wave and gesture at the air like beckoning fingers - *"I don't like this forest..."* The deep, vibrant shade of red their leaves take make it, at once, better and worse. They're beautiful to look at, all still clinging to the oaks - not one on the ground. But they also make the scene all the more alien.

As his tired eyes fall upon the village in the distance, he sits up straight upon his saddle and rubs the haze off his eyes. The smell of wood smoke and fresh bread grows stronger as he approaches the village. A young half-wolfkin child briefly waves at him as he passes, and a few merchants bark prices and offers to him, but none others pay him much mind - it was a common route to Crestbend Harbor, so passersby like himself must be commonplace.

Past the village, some distance away, he brings his mount to a stop and gets down on his feet with a sigh, looking back at the village - *"Must be far enough."* After retriving an envelope from his bag, he looks at the back, reading the instructions once more:
	*"Walk past it 'til the start of the curve, then walk into the forest to the left - the east. Walk in that direction 'til they find you."*
He ties the lead of his horse to a nearby tree and peers through the trees, seeing not very far. He sighs, feeling the cold, dry air in his nose and throat, and steps out of the road, walking into the forest.

He snakes his way around the trees and through the underbrush, eyes flitting toward every sound not coming from underfoot - hares and other critters, hopefully. Birdcalls and rustling leaves also accompany him as he ventures deeper, that distinctive smell of fallen leaves growing stronger. He takes mental notes of certain features - clumps of trees, boulders and outcrops, fallen trees - to make sure he'll know his way back to the road. The sight of one such feature elicits an immature smirk: a pair of somewhat suggestive-looking burls, on a birch tree. He continues his trek with a somewhat lighter step, not paying it much mind... until he lifts an eyebrow at the sight of another, almost identical-looking pair. And then a third. *"That's... not possible,"* he mumbles to himself, his breathing a little harder and his eyes more alive as he moves on. At the fourth, he slows to a stop, scanning his surroundings with furrowed brows. *"I really don't like this forest..."*

Somewhat suddenly, those whistles and whispers that had been accompanying him ring out louder, as if coming toward him. A shudder hits him as these sounds seem to almost pass through him and off in the opposite direction, leaving him wide-eyed and panting. In its wake, a soft voice latches onto his mind and echoes within, as if coming from everywhere and no where at once.

	— "Lost?" It teases, its unseen lips curled into an almost audible smirk.

He spins in place, his frantic gaze meeting only empty forest. His heart, already fast, now races. His palms sweat as he reaches for the sword at his hip.

	— "Where are you?" He shouts. "Are you Silverleaf?"
	— "Oh? A visitor? Courier, perhaps? Mm... You don't look like a courier."

He exhales a groan, rolling his eyes and straightening himself up, though one hand rests on his blade's sheath.

	— "Show yourself already. Got a letter; an important one, it seems."

Moments after, he hears two soft clacks and turns to see a pale, lithe figure emerging from behind a tree, their footfalls nigh-silent. They have one hand raised, with fingers ending in sharp, black-tipped claws. With white robes and flowing white hair, the only splashes of color are the vibrant blue obi around their waist, and their piercing eyes, the color of amber, returning his gaze. Their graceful, androgynous figure reminds him of the elves in the west of the continent, but three distinct features jump out at him: the slit pupils, the long, white-furred ears, and the white tails - two, he counts - swaying behind them, like ghostly flames. *"Kitsune."*

But in spite of this, he's not afraid. The glint in their eyes is playful and amused, not predatory or malicious. He relaxes somewhat, taking his hand off his scabbard to show their Everreach Order insignia, briefly.

	— "Mm, as I figured," they speak - the same voice he'd heard in his mind; also androgynous, but still noticeably masculine.
	— "Here," he puts away his insignia and hands them the letter. "I take it I won't have any problems getting back?"	
	— "Mm? You shouldn't no," they - he - replies, expression turning from amused to bemused as he reads the sender's name on the envelope. "I thank you for the delivery, and wish you safe travels."
	— "Yeah, sure... Thanks."

As the human turns back and walks away, he turns as well, still looking down at the envelope in his clawed hands. *"Seems early for another... And an Everreach finger for delivery?"* he thinks to himself with a sigh. It hadn't been long since her latest correspondence, which, as usual - unlike this one - contained a few trinkets from her studies and presents in the form of teas and herbs, in addition to a letter. As he breaks the seal, the smell of dried herbs, flowers and tea hit his nose - it almost smells like his garden in summer. He starts reading, already smirking at the first word.

<blockquote>

Ras-berry,

I hope you didn't startle the poor guy too bad. Though I guess if you did, you probably wouldn't be reading this anyway.

I know this is sudden, and you probably have a lot of things to prepare for winter by the time you get this, but... there are troublesome things happening, and something important's been worrying me, and I could... use having you around.

Okay, I... really need you here, okay? And I'm admitting it, so that you know it's important... You know how I am.

Since I know you'll be coming, please have a safe journey. And a quick one. And... probably bring some of your old clothes, *Nilas*.

Yours always,

Ella.

</blockquote>

Ras' expression as he finishes reading is one of worry and concern. *"What could warrant this?.."* Her vague mention of 'troublesome things' puzzles him. One thing is certain, however: it seems he's going on a journey.

Despite his concern, he can't help but chuckle softly reading the closing comments of her letter - he can picture her momentarily thinking she's done before thinking of another thing to add. There's even less space between those and her signature.

Despite the whirlwind of thoughts racing in his mind, he refrains from indulging in them, turning his mind to the necessary preparations for his journey - *"It shan't take much more than two weeks or so to get there, so I shouldn't need much."* As he arrives at his wooden cabin in the woods, however, it isn't with such preparations that he's concerned yet.

After grabbing a few things, he leaves home again and heads southward towards Crestbend Harbor, a large coastal city nearly an hour's walk away. Priding itself as having one of the biggest harbors in the east of the continent, many refer to it as the Pearl of the East Coast. Being such a melting pot of commerce and travel, as well as housing the only chapter of the Everreach Order in the region, it is well guarded. It's quite familiar to him, however - and him to it; enough that he's seldom inspected upon entry, a fact which he takes great advantage of. Once through the gates, he sighs, steeling himself to brave through the hustle and bustle of the market district. Thankfully his destination is at its outskirts, where it's relatively quieter.

He stops at a quaint, cozy-looking shop, built mostly from wood and clay bricks. He slows down to appreciate the sight of the many plants displayed in pots at the front, which brings a warm little smile to his lips. Making his way inside, though, he approaches the middle-aged, dark-skinned elf sat behind the counter, currently indulging on a book of some sort - quite interested, it seems, as they nearly fail to notice him.

- ― "*Ras'yi*!" Ulliver greets him by a Ley'iyish nickname.
- ― "Old man," he greets them back with a gentle smile and a nod. "I hope I'm not distracting you from your book; it certainly seems interesting," he teases.
- ― "Keeping my mind off the pain, mostly. But it is interesting. It's a pocket herbal a good client gifted me recently, from the central plains." He sets the book on the counter and turns it around with their unsteady hands.

Ulliver is a friend and fellow herbalist of him, as well as a business partner of sorts. Ras often provides them with the excesses from his garden - mostly medicinal and ornamental plants, with more pricey, rare and exotic specimens mixed in - to sell off in his stead. Almost a year prior, Ulliver became afflicted with a race-specific malady, which has been growing progressively worse. The sight of their shaking hands concerns him a fair bit, though they have been quite clear and adamant that they are not willing to seek the help of his kin to cure it.

- ― "Ooh," Ras coos, leafing through it. "Good client indeed. Consider giving them a discount on this, if they're interested," producing from his shoulder bag a small jar of dried red leaves and petals. "It's for you, though."
- ― "Crimsonthorns?" Ulliver raises their eyebrows, skeptical. "What's the occasion?"
- ― "Well... I'm leaving on what will probably be long journey," he explains, his expression dimming. "Riella sent me a letter requesting that I travel to her. Seems there might be something bad happening - I'm not sure," he says, waving his hand a bit.
- ― "Hmm... Nothing too bad, I hope. I like that girl," they say pensively.
- ― "Mhm," Ras nods in agreement before changing the subject. "In either case, I'm likely to be away for at least a month or so, I reckon... So I'll trust you with this," he says, placing a folded up piece of parchment.

Though it takes Ulliver a few moments to parse it, he finds that it contains instructions on how to find one's way through the illusion surrounding the perimeter of his home.

- ― "What, the information? Are you giving me your house?" He asks, visibly confused, at which Ras chuckles lightly.
- ― "Not giving, no. But I trust you to care for my garden in my absence, if you'd like to. And you may help yourself to the harvests until I return."

Ulliver's nonchalant expression turns to solemnity as the amount of trust being placed upon him dawns on him. Unable to come up with some witty remark, he simply nods.

- ― "Well, in that case, let me pay forward this gift," he says, pushing the pocket handbook to Ras' side of the counter. "You're going in that direction anyway. Maybe you'll find something good."

Ras feels hesitant to accept it, but knows better than to try to reject a gift from Ulliver; especially given the gift in question and the circumstances - it is nearly guaranteed to prove useful.

After conversing for some time afterwards, he bids farewell to his friend - and to the *lefenni* at the front of the shop. He takes a stroll through the Merchant Square, admiring the more exotic goods being sold by merchants from abroad, and down the wharf, gazing over the various ships moored there, peoplewatching along the way. As the sky starts turning orange, however, he leaves the city's walls one last time before his journey. Making his way back home, already past nightfall, he does his best to relax and rest in preparation for the following week.
