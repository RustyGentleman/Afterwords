# The Defiance of Creation

Though I didn't realize it in clear and explicit terms until my later years, the idea, the concept; the act and art of creation, has always been my biggest passion.

The thought of taking disparate objects, physical and/or conceptual, and working them together into something that is more than their sum.

The thought of something which the existence of, though independent from that of I, is intrinsically tied to it, and that still may exist longer than I.

The act of creation is a big fuck-you to the tyranny of time and entropy, and humanity's greatest gift, and it seems intensely fitting for it to have been my biggest passion in life, as one whose biggest fear was the concept of time itself.

## Music

I've always had a _weird_, special and intense connection to music. At the ending point of my life, some of the reasons were clear:

- As an aspie, having echolalia meant I could, when physically possible, perfectly mimic sounds and sequence of sounds I heard, and that included song vocals and note sequences - an ability which I never managed to fully grasp whether it's considered perfect pitch or not.
- As an aspie, again, my mind seemed _particularly_ well tuned to patterns, including those in music: patterns in and relations between sequences of notes, rhythms and changes in them, line structure and word choice in lyrics.
- Having chronic depression from a young age, music was sometimes the **only way** I had to be in touch with my emotions, as well as to express them, by sharing.

I never had to chance to investigate the neurology of it, but something about the high complexity and rhythmicality of genres like progressive rock and metal, jazz fusion, and baroque classical music, would make **my whole brain** light up like christmas lights, to the point of it being (and I apologize for the arguably crass wording) near-orgasmic.

tacMusic was the __only way I had__ to reliably experience intense positive feelings and emotions for the entirety of my life.

### Fathomless (2024)

tac_"A simple and relaxing text-based fishing game, with themes __excluding__ cosmic horror, psychological terror and existential horror, and an atmosphere __completely lacking__ in tension."_

...I like being cheeky with this one, but it is a really simple game, gameplay-wise - much of it is typing "fish" and pressing enter, repeatedly. But I do feel like its simplicity can be misleading, and there are other elements that I hoped would keep a player's curiosity. I did also put a lot of effort into the sound design, and had a fair bit of fun looking through <a href="https://freesound.org/">Freesound</a>, picking things and editing them.

This is how I personally view the _"lore"_ of this game: ||The player character has, in some way, become fused with a research submarine vessel that had been tasked with investigating some weird occurrences and findings in a specific area of the ocean. At the start, it's less obvious, but it grows more evident as the game progresses and events happen - with one event literally equating the vessel's hull to the player's skin, if I remember correctly.||

I remember I'd planned on eventually expanding on it, adding specific fish species, more events with visual effects and cues, that sort of deal.

### Retroweb 2000s (2025)

tacSo, this is a big, special one, and requires some background.

I was part of a streaming group and community called the Goblin Horde, which I helped a friend start, build and maintain. A group of us, her friends, at some point, started gathering every year to to put something together for her birthday - slides with gifs and inside jokes, that sort of thing - and, at some point, we started passing around the "crown", or the group's leadership, along with the responsibility to plan the thing.

In 2025, the crown was mine, and I decided we'd be making a simple little website for her - with me doing the brunt of the work, it being my idea. >>~~It feels like I'm bragging, but I'm really not.~~<< The initial idea was simple: an early-2000s-internet themed website, with a home page and a personal page for each person in the group that wanted one.

tac...That is... not what it ended up being.

What followed was an insanely hectic and fun month of programming, image and audio editing, idea workshopping, troubleshooting, and whatever else. What the initial idea promised was delivered, but the project also grew into essentially a point-and-click game, where each page included interactive elements that would, in some way, award the player a key. Collecting all keys and unlocking a door in the home page led to an "ending" page, featuring birthday messages and gifs with inside jokes and all that. It also included a Clippy-like in-website "assistant" that would comment on and react to a few things, a handful of different dialogue systems - each for a different purpose, in a different style, and to meet different needs - a crafting/alchemy system, a media player where songs are unlocked as the player explores, a monster-catching _"minigame_", and many interactive elements and little details.

Sadly, our friend braved the whole event without shedding a single tear, to our eternal shame and dishonor.
